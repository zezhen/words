Happy Mid-Autumn Festival

Sep 26, 2015 | englishessay

The Mid-Autumn Festival is a harvest festival celebrated by ethnic Chinese people. The festival is held on the 15th day of the eighth month in the Chinese Han calendar, on the night of the full moon between early September to early October of the Gregorian calendar. The Mid-Autumn Festival is one of the four most important Chinese festivals.

Mooncake (simplified Chinese: 月饼; traditional Chinese: 月餅; pinyin: yuè bĭng) is a Chinese bakery product traditionally eaten during the Mid-Autumn Festival (Zhongqiujie). The festival is for lunar worship and moon watching, when mooncakes are regarded as an indispensable delicacy. Mooncakes are offered between friends or on family gatherings while celebrating the festival.

Chinese people give the full moon whose shape is roundness as the meaning of family together, when I was in China, my family will get together to have dinner and enjoy the big full moon. It’s my first time far away from my hometown at this day, fortunately I have some Chinese friends here and we can have funs and celebration together.

Happy Mid-Autumn Festival!
